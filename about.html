About Me

My name is Brandon Seymour, and I recently completed my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science at Southern New Hampshire University while working full-time as a Senior Desktop Support Analyst in healthcare IT. My journey into technology has been anything but traditional, but it has given me a unique perspective and a strong appreciation for continuous learning and professional growth.

Before beginning my career in information technology, I served in the United States Marine Corps as a helicopter mechanic. During my time in the military, I learned the importance of teamwork, accountability, attention to detail, and performing under pressure. Those experiences continue to influence how I approach both my professional responsibilities and personal goals today.

After transitioning from military service, I pursued opportunities in information technology and have worked in a variety of technical support, systems administration, networking, and infrastructure-related roles. My current position allows me to support enterprise systems, troubleshoot complex technical issues, and work closely with users and technical teams across the organization. While my professional experience has primarily been focused on IT operations and infrastructure, earning my Computer Science degree allowed me to expand my knowledge into software engineering, algorithms and data structures, databases, cybersecurity, software testing, and application development.

This ePortfolio showcases my growth throughout the Computer Science program and highlights my ability to analyze, improve, and enhance existing software solutions. The artifacts included throughout this portfolio demonstrate my technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and commitment to producing maintainable and effective solutions. My goal is to continue building upon this foundation as I pursue future opportunities in software engineering, systems engineering, cybersecurity, and technology leadership.

Outside of work and technology, I enjoy spending time with my wife, and our two children. Family has always been a major source of motivation for me, and completing my degree while balancing career and family responsibilities is an accomplishment that I am particularly proud of. I believe that learning never truly stops, and I look forward to continuing to grow both professionally and personally throughout my career.
