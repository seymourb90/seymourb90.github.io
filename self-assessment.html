Professional Self-Assessment

Completing the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University has been one of the most rewarding accomplishments of my professional and personal life. My path to earning this degree was not a traditional one. After serving in the United States Marine Corps as a helicopter mechanic, I began college, changed majors, moved states, took time away from school, and eventually returned to finish what I started. Balancing a full-time career, family responsibilities, and coursework required discipline, persistence, and strong time management skills.

Throughout the Computer Science program, I strengthened my technical knowledge while building on the experience I gained through my career in information technology. As a Senior Desktop Support Analyst, I regularly troubleshoot complex technical issues, support enterprise systems, document processes, and work directly with end users across the organization. The Computer Science curriculum expanded my understanding beyond infrastructure and support by introducing software engineering principles, algorithms and data structures, databases, secure coding practices, software testing, and application development.

One of the most valuable skills I developed throughout the program was collaboration. During my military service, teamwork was critical to maintaining aircraft readiness and completing missions safely. In my current role, collaboration involves working with other IT teams, vendors, providers, nurses, operational staff, and end users to solve problems and implement technology solutions. Throughout my coursework, I learned that successful software projects require input from multiple stakeholders and that understanding different perspectives is essential when designing and implementing technical solutions.

Communication has also been a significant area of growth throughout the program. Whether documenting procedures, creating project proposals, performing code reviews, writing software design documentation, or explaining technical concepts to non-technical users, effective communication is critical. The assignments completed throughout the Computer Science program strengthened my ability to communicate technical information clearly through written, visual, and oral formats.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio demonstrate growth across several key areas of computer science. The first enhancement focuses on software design and engineering principles. Through refactoring and modularization, I improved the organization, readability, and maintainability of the original project. This enhancement reinforced the importance of designing software that is scalable, reusable, and easier to maintain over time.

The second enhancement focuses on algorithms and data structures. By introducing structured object management through the use of a SceneObject data structure and vector-based storage, I improved how objects were organized and processed within the application. This enhancement demonstrated the value of selecting appropriate data structures to reduce redundancy, improve scalability, and create more maintainable solutions.

The third enhancement focuses on databases and data management concepts. Instead of relying on hardcoded object definitions, the application was modified to load scene information from an external CSV file. This data-driven approach separates data from application logic and improves flexibility, maintainability, and future scalability.

Security has become an increasingly important aspect of my technical skillset throughout both my education and professional career. Courses such as CS 305 Secure Coding introduced me to vulnerability assessments, dependency analysis, encryption, secure software development practices, and risk mitigation strategies. These concepts reinforced the importance of designing systems that are not only functional but also resilient against potential threats. In my professional IT responsibilities, security considerations are present every day through access management, system administration, endpoint protection, and data protection.

Another important takeaway from the Computer Science program has been the ability to analyze and improve existing solutions rather than simply creating new ones. Real-world technology environments often involve maintaining, enhancing, and supporting systems that already exist. The enhancement process used throughout this capstone project required analysis, planning, implementation, testing, and documentation, which closely mirrors the type of work performed in professional technology environments.

Taken together, the artifacts in this ePortfolio represent my growth across software engineering, algorithms and data structures, databases, communication, collaboration, and security. The code review demonstrates my ability to evaluate existing software and identify opportunities for improvement. The three enhancements demonstrate my ability to apply computer science concepts to improve software design, implement structured solutions, and create data-driven applications.

This ePortfolio represents more than a collection of academic projects. It reflects years of personal growth, professional experience, and dedication to completing a goal that I set for myself many years ago. Earning a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science is an important milestone, but it is also the foundation for future growth. I look forward to continuing to expand my technical skills, pursuing new challenges, and applying the knowledge gained through both my career and education to solve meaningful problems in the technology field.
